Rights of Students

Subject to limitations prescribed by law and the University policies and regulations, every student of the University shall enjoy the following rights:

Section 1. The right to admission without undue discrimination and/or the right freely to choose his/her fields of study subject to existing curricula and to continue his/her course up to graduation except in case of academic deficiency or violation of disciplinary regulations, misbehavior that may warrant his expulsion from the University.

Section 2. The right to competent instruction and relevant quality education through provision of adequate welfare services and academic facilities to the financial capacity of the school.

Section 3. The right to organize student organizations, student council/government and to be accredited and granted of privileges accorded to such organizations.

Section 4. The right to vote and to run for any elective position contested in the SSC election subject to the provisions of the Constitution and By-Laws of the SSC.

Section 5. The right to form Student Commission on Elections (COMELEC) in coordination with the office of the Student Services of the Campus, and once constituted, it shall be independent and autonomous from any sector of the school and shall ensure the conduct of clean, honest and peaceful elections.

Section 6. Prohibition in the use of force and exaction od excessive fees from their members as well as their other student activities shall be considered and implemented by all campus organizations.

Section 7. The right to be informed on matters directly or indirectly affecting their welfare or on the rules and regulations of the school affecting them.

Section 8. The right to freedom of expression - to freely express their beliefs, views and opinions, provided, however, that the exercise of such will not disrupt or interfere in the administrative, academic and discipline of the University.

Section 9.The right to publish student newspaper and other similar publications, eir pages materials that they deem in pursuance of their academic freedom and freedom of expression in accordance with the existing U es on publication and with Campus Journalism Act of 1991.

Section 10. The right to due process (the right to defend himself, to be heard and to present evidence on his/her behalf before an impartial body) before being subjected to disciplinary proceedings/action.

Section 11. The right to have access to school records and issuance of officia certificates, the confidentiality of which shall be maintained and preserved by the University.

Section 12. The right to peaceful assembly and petition the school authorities for the redress of their grievances in manners within the limits of the University rules and regulations and other existing laws.

Section 13. The right to be free from compulsory involuntary contributions and sale of benefit tickets either directly or indirectly by any person, organization or entity from within and outside of the University except when the same have been approved by their own student organizations, in which case, the collection and disposal of the collections is done by the organization.

Section 14. The right to academic freedom, which shall consist of, but not limited to, the following:

a. The right to be free from compulsory assignments not related to his subjects/courses as defined in the course syllabus.

b.The right to be free from involuntary servitude as a requirement for the completion of his academic deficiencies. c. The right to hold co-curricular activities and other affairs not inimical to the interest of the University.

d. The right to conduct researches in connection with academic work, an to freely discuss and publish their findings and recommendations.

e. The right to conduct inquiry within the campus in the curricular and extra-curricular activities.

f. The right to choose a field of study and to pursue the quest for truth; to directly or indirectly affects the students of the University.

g. The right to invite off-campus speakers or resource persons, to student sponsored assemblies, fora, symposia, and other similar activities. opinion on any subject or public or general concern which

h. The right to express contrary interpretations or dissenting opinions inside and outside the classroom

i. The right to participate in the drafting of a new curriculum and in the review or revision of the old.

j. The right to participate in the drafting and/or revising of the student handbook which shall include the school rules and regulations, a copy of which shall be furnished the students upon admission to the school; and

k. The right to be free from any form of indoctrination leading to imposed ideological hegemony.

Section 15. The right to receive reasonable protection within the University premises during school days and on any day when they are undertaking any activity cleared with appropriate University authorities.

Section 16. The right to file an impeachment to any student leader who is incapable and irresponsible of holding such a position and found guilty of wrong doings at least 50% plus one complainants.

Section 17. The right against unreasonable searches and seizures, and the right to privacy of communication and correspondence.

Section 18. The right to be assured of all other rights granted by the laws of the land.
